# Color Green

## University-Level Analysis

Green is the color of light with wavelengths between approximately 495–570 nm. It is perceived by the human eye when the medium-wavelength cone cells (M-cones) are stimulated more than the long- and short-wavelength cones. In physics, green is a primary additive color in the RGB color model.

## Biological Significance

Green is abundant in nature due to chlorophyll, the pigment that enables photosynthesis in plants. The human visual system is most sensitive to green light, a trait likely evolved to detect foliage and ripe fruit.

## Cultural and Symbolic Roles

In many cultures, green symbolizes growth, fertility, and safety. It is used as a signal for 'go' in traffic lights and as the color of environmental movements. In art, green often represents tranquility and nature.

## Perception and Psychology

Green is commonly associated with balance, calmness, and restfulness. Studies indicate that exposure to green spaces can reduce stress and improve cognitive function.
